Fun Educational Games

As the old adage goes: “All work and no play makes Jack a dull boy.” While it is vital for a student to put in the necessary effort, and receive
help when needed, students cannot study all day every day, as they are not robots. However, even a student’s break time can still hold
educational value! To help students across the globe retain their love for learning, we’ve devised a number of fun learning activities, which keep
students active and away from their screens!

Math Games

One and One: This is based on a common term used in amateur basketball, in which the person shooting free throws faces the following situation: if the
player misses, the ball is live. Otherwise he/she gets another shot. We decided to apply this to our game as well, but instead of
shooting free throws, we’re doing math problems.

For this exercise, you need a clean trash can and a crumpled paper ball. Place the can 15 feet from each competitor. For each turn,
have the player solve a math-related problem. If he/she gets the problem correct, the player gets one point, and gets one chance to
shoot the paper ball into the trash can. The player must sit down in a criss cross stance,If he/she makes the shot, he gets one more
point. However, if the player gets the problem wrong, he/she gets zero points, and their turn is over. First to get 10(or any other number)
points is declared the winner!

Vocabulary & Spelling Games

Modified Shiritori : Inspired by the traditional Japanese word game, Shiritori, each player must say a word that starts with the ending letter of the last word. However, we’ve added some twists to this Japanese past time. Each person has 10-15 seconds to think of a word that starts
with the ending letter of the last word, and it has to be unique from the previous words. If the player fails to meet either of these
requirements, they would receive a strike. If a player gets 3 strikes, they are eliminated. The last man standing is the winner!

Grammar Games

Squirt Gun Mania: Looking for a way to spice up grammar with a high-stakes yet kid-friendly game? Well this is the one for you!
For this game, you’d need one squirt gun.
For this exercise, have each competitor fix a sentence so that it becomes grammatically correct. For each error a player gets, he/she gains one point, and gets shot with the water gun one time by the host. If the contestant gets the sentence completely correct, he/she gets to shoot the host one time with the water gun! If a player gets 10 (or any number) points, they are eliminated! The last man standing is the winner!
